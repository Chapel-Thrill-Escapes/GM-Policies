Employee Policies
The following Policies apply to all employees of Chapel Thrill Escapes. Any three violations of these
Policies will result in employee termination or suspension. Any individual violation of these Policies may
be grounds for employee termination or suspension. These Policies are not intended to be, nor should be
they construed to be, an enumeration of all behavior prohibited to, or expected of, employees of Chapel
Thrill Escapes. Unprofessional or otherwise unacceptable conduct of a category not explicitly mentioned
in the Policies below may be grounds for employee termination or suspension.
1. Employees must respect the escape room premises in Cobb Residence Hall as property of the
University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Employees must also respect all properties and assets
leased, owned, or otherwise used by Chapel Thrill Escapes. Employees must act with due diligence to
ensure that such assets and properties (hereafter referred to as “the workplace”) remain in good
condition. Intentionally causing damage to the escape room’s interior, as well as any other part of the
workplace, is prohibited. If damage to any part of the workplace occurs during the course of escape
room operations, employees must notify a member of the Executive Team
3
as soon as possible.
2. Employees must be punctual. Employees that are unable to arrive at the workplace before their
scheduled shift time or cannot work a scheduled shift must notify a member of the Executive Team as
soon as possible. Except in the case of an emergency, such notice must be given at least twenty-four
hours in advance of the scheduled shift.
3. Employees must report hours consistent with the amount of time they have worked. Clocking in
before arriving at the workplace or clocking out after leaving the workplace is prohibited. If time
tracking issues arise, employees must notify a member of the Executive Team as soon as possible.
4. Employees must not enter the workplace under the inﬂuence of alcohol or other drugs. Consumption
of such substances is also prohibited in the workplace.
5. Employees must maintain a neat, well-groomed appearance at all times within the workplace. Clothing
depicting profane language or symbols and revealing clothing are prohibited. Employees are
encouraged, but not required to wear casual attire.
6. Employees must not enter the workplace outside of their scheduled working hours for any reason
other than an escape room booking without explicit permission from a member of the Executive Team.
7. Employees authorized to utilize social media accounts managed by Chapel Thrill Escapes must limit
their use of such accounts to activities authorized within the Procedures or by express permission of a

Employee Procedures
The following Procedures are intended as a sequential outline of the tasks that employees of Chapel Thrill
Escapes are expected to complete during their scheduled working hours. These Procedures may be
expanded, reduced, or otherwise modiﬁed at any time by any member of the Executive Team to reﬂect the
administrative needs of Chapel Thrill Escapes, its members, or its Executive Team.
1. The employee arrives at the workplace on time. Upon arrival, they clock in, retrieve a single set of
building keys from the exterior lockbox, and unlock the door to the escape room.
2. The employee removes all unneeded items from the escape room, including the merchandise rack and
the Gamemaster table, and relocates them to the corridor. These items should be arranged to
maintain a safe environment for employees and guests.
3. The employee surveys the room according to the Room Reset section of the Room Protocol document,
ensuring that all puzzles have been properly reset by the last employee to close the escape room. If
any items are damaged or otherwise disturbed, the employee should notify a member of the Executive
Team as soon as possible.
4. The employee ensures that the escape room’s lights, fan, and dehumidiﬁer are turned on. If necessary,
the employee empties the dehumidiﬁer’s excess water.
5. The employee logs in to the company tablet and ensures that the cameras and microphones inside the
escape room are functioning. They begin ﬁlling out a Gamemaster Log to document their shift.

Employee Procedures
The following Procedures are intended as a sequential outline of the tasks that employees of Chapel Thrill
Escapes are expected to complete during their scheduled working hours. These Procedures may be
expanded, reduced, or otherwise modiﬁed at any time by any member of the Executive Team to reﬂect the
administrative needs of Chapel Thrill Escapes, its members, or its Executive Team.
1. The employee arrives at the workplace on time. Upon arrival, they clock in, retrieve a single set of
building keys from the exterior lockbox, and unlock the door to the escape room.
2. The employee removes all unneeded items from the escape room, including the merchandise rack and
the Gamemaster table, and relocates them to the corridor. These items should be arranged to
maintain a safe environment for employees and guests.
3. The employee surveys the room according to the Room Reset section of the Room Protocol document,
ensuring that all puzzles have been properly reset by the last employee to close the escape room. If
any items are damaged or otherwise disturbed, the employee should notify a member of the Executive
Team as soon as possible.
4. The employee ensures that the escape room’s lights, fan, and dehumidiﬁer are turned on. If necessary,
the employee empties the dehumidiﬁer’s excess water.
5. The employee logs in to the company tablet and ensures that the cameras and microphones inside the
escape room are functioning. They begin ﬁlling out a Gamemaster Log to document their shift.

6. The employee awaits the arrival of guests for their scheduled escape room booking. Guests should be
professionally greeted at the top of the staircase to the workplace and led to the corridor adjacent to
the escape room. Employees should politely introduce themselves by name and welcome guests to
Chapel Thrill Escapes. If guests are awaiting the arrival of the remainder of their party, employees
should try to make casual conversation and avoid awkward silence.
7. Once all guests have arrived, the employee invites them to place their shoes in the cubby and
welcomes them into the escape room. Once all guests have entered the room, the employee closes the
door and reads the Room Intro and General Reminders given in the Room Protocol document.
8. The employee asks whether guests have any questions before their time begins. They answer any
questions that arise, then exit the room, shut the door, and begin a timer on the company tablet.
9. The employee guides guests through the escape room according to the Room Flow section in the
Room Protocol document. They should follow along with guests’ actions in order to best assist them; if
unsure about guests’ progress, the employee should ask what guests have tried and which puzzle they
are working on. If guests appear to be stuck in a dead end for more than ﬁve minutes, the employee
should give them a hint to get them back on the right track.
10. Once guests have collected all potion bottles and inserted them into the centrifuge, the employee
presses the wired button hanging from the left side of the corridor and plays the audio ﬁle of the
Duke’s challenge to the guests.
11. If guests complete the escape room within the allotted time, the employee enters the room and
congratulates them on their success. The employee should highlight this feat and inform them of the
time that it took them to complete the room.
12. If guests do not complete the escape room, the employee enters the room and thanks the players for
coming in. Instead of focusing on the guests’ failure, the employee should emphasize their success and
may praise or “hype up” speciﬁc actions taken during the guests’ allotted time.
13. The employee asks guests if they would like to be featured on Chapel Thrill Escapes’ social media
channels. If they would, the employee takes a picture of them with the company tablet and uploads it
to the Story of the Chapel Thrill Escapes Instagram account with an appropriate caption, the guests’
selected team name, and any requested account mentions. The same image of the guests should be
submitted through the Gamemaster Log.
14. The employee thanks guests for visiting Chapel Thrill Escapes and the Rameses in Wonderland escape
room. The employee informs guests that leaving a review on Google will reward them with a bonus.
15. The employee works through the escape room according to the Reset Protocol. All puzzles within the
escape room should be reset to their initial state and lock combinations should be scrambled.
16. If the employee is working the last shift of the day, they vacuum the ﬂoor of both sections of the
escape room. Care should be taken to avoid disturbing the green turf within the ﬁrst room, as well as
any loose wires.
17. After completing all Procedures, the employee turns off the light, clocks out, locks the door to the
escape room, and replaces the building keys within the exterior lockbox.
